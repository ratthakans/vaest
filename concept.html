The whole field is racing in one direction — faster, cheaper, more capable. VÆST is built to move somewhere else entirely: toward understanding the things that can't be benchmarked.

The race everyone is running

Intelligence has become a speed contest

Every few weeks a new model is faster, cheaper, longer-context, higher on the benchmark. The competition is real, and it's almost entirely about the measurable: tokens per second, problems solved, tools called, reasoning steps. It's an extraordinary race — and it has almost nothing to say about whether the work is any good.

Efficiency is not the same as taste.

Two different axes

What each one is actually optimizing

The industry axis

Faster · cheaper · more capable

  • Tokens per second and time to first token
  • Benchmark scores and reasoning depth
  • Context length and tool orchestration
  • Agents, automation, throughput at scale
  • Answers that are correct
The VÆST axis

More tasteful · more considered · more human

  • Cultural fluency — what a nuance signals to whom
  • Mood, tone and the emotional read of a choice
  • The judgment to cut the banal and keep the brave
  • Answers that are good
Aesthetic Intelligence

The dimensions VÆST is built to understand

The abstract, the felt, the almost-unsayable — the parts of creative work that a spreadsheet can't hold. This is the territory VÆST is trained and tuned for.

Art
The references, movements and craft a piece lives inside — and why one composition sings while another just sits there.
Aesthetic
Proportion, restraint, rhythm, the confidence of a choice — the difference between designed and merely decorated.
Culture
What a word, colour or gesture means in a place and a moment — the fluency to be relevant without being a cliché.
Emotion
What a piece is trying to make someone feel — and whether every element is actually pulling toward that feeling.
Mood & Tone
The register a whole document speaks in — warm, austere, playful, precise — and holding it consistent from line to line.
Taste
The decision underneath all of it: this stays, this goes. The cut a creative director makes — and the one VÆST learns from you.
